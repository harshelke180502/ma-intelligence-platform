Developer Skills Assessment
M&A Thesis Research Tool with Dashboard

Overview
The Exit Group is a boutique M&A advisory firm that sources acquisition targets for private
equity clients. A core part of our work involves identifying companies that match a specific
investment thesis (industry, geography, size, ownership type) and presenting those companies
to our PE clients for evaluation.
This project simulates that workflow. Your goal is to build a tool that takes an M&A thesis as
input, researches and collects companies that fit the criteria, and presents those companies in a
clean, functional dashboard with key performance indicators.
This is not a trick question or an abstract puzzle. We want to see how you approach a real
business problem end to end.

The Thesis
Use the following M&A thesis for your project. All candidates will work from this same thesis so
we can evaluate results consistently.

Target Thesis: Specialty Tax Advisory Services
Our PE client has acquired a specialty tax consulting platform (focused on cost segregation
and engineering based tax studies) and is looking to build a holistic specialty tax advisory
firm through add on acquisitions. The thesis is to find privately held specialty tax firms
across the Continental US that provide one or more of the following services:
• R&D Tax Credits
• Cost Segregation
• Work Opportunity Tax Credits (WOTC)
• Sales & Use Tax consulting
Size threshold: Estimated revenue greater than $3M or employee count greater than 5.
Geography: Continental United States.
Ownership: Privately held. Not affiliated with a union workforce.
Exclusions: Do not include companies whose primary service is Employee Retention
Credit (ERC) work, and do not include firms focused exclusively on Property Tax consulting.

Why This Thesis
This is a real search mandate. The PE backed platform already has a strong cost segregation
practice and a 200+ person sales team generating deal flow. They are acquiring firms that add
R&D credit capabilities, Sales & Use tax expertise, and WOTC services to create a one stop
specialty tax advisory platform. The companies you find in this project are the same types of
companies we research every day.

What to Build
Your deliverable is a working application with two parts: a data pipeline and a dashboard.

Part 1: Data Pipeline
Build a backend process that collects company data matching the thesis above. You can pull
from any publicly available sources (Google Maps API, state business filings, industry
directories, professional association member lists, LinkedIn, web scraping, etc.). For each
company, try to capture as many of the following fields as possible:

Company name
Location (city, state)
Website URL
Primary service(s) offered (R&D Credits, Cost Seg, WOTC, Sales & Use Tax, or
multiple)
Estimated revenue (if available)
Employee count (if available)
Ownership type (private, franchise, PE backed, etc.)
Key contact or owner name (if publicly listed)
Data source (where you found this company)

We do not expect 100% field coverage on every company. What matters is how you handle
incomplete data, deduplication, and data quality. Specialty tax is a niche industry so the
universe of targets is smaller than a broad services search. Finding 30 to 80 quality, relevant
companies would be a strong result.

Part 2: Dashboard
Build a web based dashboard that presents the collected companies. The dashboard should
include:
•
•

•
•

A sortable, filterable table of all companies found
KPI summary cards at the top (total companies found, breakdown by service type,
geographic distribution by state, percentage with identified ownership, average
estimated revenue, etc.)
At least one chart or visual (service type distribution, geographic heat map, revenue
distribution, or similar)
Filter by service type (R&D Credits, Cost Seg, WOTC, Sales & Use Tax)
The ability to click into a company row and see all available detail

Design does not need to be award winning. Functional, clean, and intuitive beats flashy.

What Happens Next
After submission, we will review your project and schedule a 30 to 45 minute technical
walkthrough. During that call, we will ask you to demo the application, walk us through your
architecture and decisions, and explain how you would extend or improve it with more time. The
walkthrough carries as much weight as the code itself.
